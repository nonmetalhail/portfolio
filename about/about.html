Hi, I’m Elliot! I’m an architect turned building scientist turned information scientist. I guess that makes me part designer, part scientist, and part engineer.

A California native, I studied architecture in college. After college, I worked as an architect, and later a construction cost estimator. While I worked in the building industry, I developed a strong desire to make the built environment a more sustainable place. In pursuit of this goal, I began a MS program in Building Science believing that research and knowledge is key in implementing change.

While in grad school, I became interested in building energy dashboards and how information, when presented thoughtfully, can influence people’s behavior and act to conserve resources. This interest served me later as I applied my broad set of skills working at a non-profit, the Institute for the Sustainable Performance of Buildings (Superb). At Superb, I aided in the development of educational software intended to teach building professionals the basics of high performance buildings.

My interest in information visualization and experience developing educational software then led me to the I School where I have found my calling in User Experience design.

The process of UX design resonates with me. In architectural design, the emphasis is more often than not about space and voids, guided by the architect’s own hubris, rather than a clear focus on the user’s needs. As someone who wanted to design things for people, the architect’s focus was counter-intuitive. I’m glad to finally be in a place where others share similar perspectives on design.

When not harping on architects, I enjoy the hiking-photography nexus, cooking delicious meals, baking bread and fancy cakes, nerding out over TED talks, and playing my guitar or listening to music (from bluegrass to the symphony).
